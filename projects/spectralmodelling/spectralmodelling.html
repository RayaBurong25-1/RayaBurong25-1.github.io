21M.080: Low Resolution Spectral Modelling Synthesis

code | presentation

Who

This is a project made during the 21M.080 (Introduction to Music Technology) class in Fall 2019. It was a solo final project.

What

This project attempts to recreate the sound of musical instruments using a variant of short-time Fourier transform (CQT, Constant-Q Transform), a low number of sine waves and genetic algorithms.

Where

This project was done on MIT campus during the last couple weeks of the semester.

When

26 November 2020 - 12 December 2020 (2 weeks)

Why

It's a fun little project to demonstrate knowledge in music technology, and for myself to see how easy or difficult we can synthesize musical instrument sounds using additive synthesis. Timbre plays a huge role in our perception of sounds, as I would learn later in fall 2020 while taking 6.003. The results show that spectral modelling synthesis takes more effort than it looks. As with any genetic algorithm, the fitness function plays a significant role in how the results fare to the human ears - a "better" fitness function would make the resulting sound "better".

How

I wrote the entirety of the project in Python, including the genetic algorithm parts but excluding the sound synthesis and CQT (Constant-Q Transform), which I used the librosa library.
